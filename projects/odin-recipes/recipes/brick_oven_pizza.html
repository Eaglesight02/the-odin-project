Brick-Oven Pizza (Brooklyn style)

Description

Brooklyn-style Brick-Oven Pizza is an iconic culinary delight, known for its thin, crispy crust and flavorful toppings. Hand-tossed dough is stretched to perfection before being adorned with tangy tomato sauce, premium mozzarella cheese, and a variety of fresh ingredients. Cooked in a traditional brick oven at high temperatures, the pizza develops a charred, blistered crust that adds depth to every bite. The aroma of bubbling cheese and smoky dough fills the air, enticing diners with the promise of a savory feast. With each slice, you experience a perfect balance of textures and flavors, transporting you to the bustling streets of Brooklyn with every savory bite.

Ingredients

  • Dough:
    • 1/4 cup warm water
    • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
    • 1 cup cold water
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 3 cups bread flour
    • 1 tablespoon extra virgin oil

  • Toppings:
    • 6 ounces moisture mozzarella cheese, thinly sliced.
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
    • 1/2 cup no salt added canned crushed tomatoes
    • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
    • 6 leaves fresh basil, torn

  • Directions:

    • Step 1:
      Make the dough: Pour warm water into a large bowl, then sprinkle yeast over the top. Let it stand for 5 minutes. Stir in cold water and salt. Stir in flour, 1 cup at a time until incorporated.

    • Step 2:
      Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth, about 10 minutes.

    • Step 3:
      Divide in half and form it into two tight balls. Coat with olive oil and refrigerate in a sealed container for at-least 16 hours. Be sure to use a big enough container to allow the dough to rise.

    • Step 4:
      Make the pizzas: Remove the dough from the refrigerator one hour prior to rising.

    • Step 5:
      Preheat the oven with a pizza stone on the lowest rack, to 550 degrees F (288 degrees C). Lightly dust a pizza peel with flour.

    • Step 7:
      Lay 1/2 of the mozzarella on the crust, then sprinkle 1/4 teaspoon oregano and 1/8 teaspoon pepper over the top. Randomly arrange 1/4 cup crushed tomatoes over the pizza, leaving some empty areas, then drizzle 1 tablespoon olive oil over the top.

    • Step 8:
      With a quick back and forth jerk, make sure the dough will release from the peel easily. Place the tip of the peel at the back of the preheated pizza stone, and remove the peel so that the pizza is left on the stone.

    • Step 9:
      Bake in the preheated oven until the crust begins to show brown color, 4 to 6 minutes.

    • Step 10:
      Slide the pizza onto the peel and remove from the oven; then slide off the peel ont a work surface. Sprinkle 1/2 of the basil leaves randomly on the pizza. Cut into wedges and serve.

    • Step 11:
      Follow steps 5 to 9 to make the second pizza.
